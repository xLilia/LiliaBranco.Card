I'm an indie video game developer !

I've had a very strong drive for expressing my inner worlds and stories since forever.

I started exploring video game creation on my own as a kid using (unexpectedly) PowerPoint! I played with the software as if it was another game on my WindowsXP PC and built a small set of "choose your own adventure" games with ClipArt characters animated by powerpoint sequence animations and page transitions. Life was good.

Growing up, through my school years, I scribbled ideas for games and virtual worlds during class. On the 5th grade I found out about "Scratch" and had my first taste of Visual Programming. Eventually taught myself how to write small games in 2D and 3D using "GameMaker" in the 7th grade.

Through Highschool I taught myself javascript & html, C, C++, Java, Python and a bit of 3D modeling and decided to follow my dream by pursuing a bachelor degree in "Game Design & Development" at university.

For the university's final year I went to Finland for 5 months in an erasmus program, and for the final project I wrote a small game engine/framework (_Nameless), from the ground up, using C++ & OpenGL, which was graded 19/20. I was happy.

The 4 years that followed were quite hectic. I entered the job market and got myself a job at a prestigious "Big Name Banking Software Company". For my surprise it wasn't as dreamy. Whenever I had the time and energy at the end of the day or vacations, I would hone my skills at Game Programming & Design, Software & Graphics Engineering, 3D Art from modeling to rigging, writing stories and composing music for my small prototypes and projects, in an attempt to eventually go full Indie.

At the job, I became Team Lead for the duration of a year but decided to leave the position, in search for something more meaningfull.

Did some soul searching, traveled to Germany with my girlfriend, found a lot of meaning in Virtual Reality, started working on my new (Comphi) Game Engine with VR in mind, and built some VR prototypes in Unity and Unreal.

And for now I'm looking forward to continue supporting my dream while working at the VideoGames Industry.
